## Expansions FAQ

### What is the Basic Island in Sunflower Land?
The Basic Island is the first land expansion available at Prestige 0 in Sunflower Land. It unlocks progressively based on Bumpkin levels, with each stage requiring specific resources and time to complete. Expanding the Basic Island increases farming capacity and resource nodes, making it the foundation for early-game progression.

**Stage-by-stage unlocks:**
- **Bumpkin Level 1:** Unlocks Land 4, requires 3 wood, takes 5 seconds, provides 3 wood nodes.
- **Bumpkin Level 5:** Unlocks Land 5, requires 0.25 coins, takes 5 seconds, adds +8 crop plots (17 total), +1 tree (6 total), +1 stone rock (4 total), +1 iron rock (2 total), +1 gold rock (1 total).
- **Bumpkin Level 6:** Unlocks Land 6, requires 60 coins, takes 1 minute, adds +8 crop plots (25 total), +1 tree (7 total), +1 stone rock (5 total).
- **Bumpkin Level 7:** Unlocks Land 7, requires 5 coins, takes 30 minutes, adds +2 crop plots (27 total), +1 tree (8 total), +1 stone rock (6 total), +1 iron rock (3 total).
- **Bumpkin Level 8:** Unlocks Land 8, requires 3 coins, takes 4 hours, adds +2 crop plots (29 total), +1 tree (9 total), +1 stone rock (7 total), +1 gold rock (2 total).
- **Bumpkin Level 9:** Unlocks Land 9, requires 100 coins, 40 stone, and 5 iron, takes 12 hours, adds +2 crop plots (31 total), finalizing maximum capacity.

**Final Expansion Capacity:**
- 31 crop plots  
- 9 trees  
- 7 stone rocks  
- 4 iron rocks  
- 2 gold rocks  

**Cumulative Requirements:** 60.25 coins, 108 wood, 46 stone, 9 iron, and 1 gold.  
**Total Expansion Time:** Approximately 16 hours and 31 minutes.

**Irreversible:** Once the Basic Island is fully expanded, the maximum number of plots and resource nodes is fixed and cannot be changed.

### How do expansions work in Sunflower Land?
Expansions in Sunflower Land unlock progressively through Bumpkin levels and Prestige milestones. Each expansion adds new land, crop plots, and resource nodes, while later islands introduce advanced features such as flower beds, oil drills, gem mines, and lava pools. Unlocking expansions requires coins, wood, stone, iron, and gold, along with specific time durations. Once an island is fully expanded, its maximum capacity is fixed and cannot be changed.

**Expansion progression path:**
- **Basic Island (Prestige 0):** Unlocks first, provides crop plots, trees, and basic ores.
- **Petal Paradise:** Unlocks after Basic Island, introduces flower beds and expanded farming.
- **Desert Island:** Unlocks after Petal Paradise, adds oil drills and gem mines.
- **Volcano Island:** Unlocks after Desert Island, introduces lava pools and rare volcanic resources.

**Key mechanics:**
- **Bumpkin Levels:** Each expansion stage requires a specific Bumpkin level to unlock.
- **Prestige:** Determines which islands are available; Prestige 0 starts with Basic Island.
- **Resources & Time:** Each stage requires coins and materials, plus a set expansion time.
- **Finality:** Once expanded, the maximum plots and nodes are permanent.

**Irreversible:** Expansion limits are permanent; once an island is fully expanded, its maximum plots and nodes cannot be altered.

### What is the Prestige system in Sunflower Land?
Prestige in Sunflower Land is a progression reset mechanic that allows players to restart their farming journey after reaching certain milestones. By entering Prestige mode, a player resets their land expansions, crops, and resources, but gains permanent Prestige benefits that unlock new islands, features, and advanced gameplay. Prestige is designed to extend long-term progression and keep farming challenging and rewarding.

**Key details:**
- **Unlock Requirement:** Prestige becomes available after reaching specific farming levels and completing prior islands.
- **Reset Effect:** All land expansions, crops, and resources are reset to the starting state.
- **Permanent Benefits:** Prestige unlocks new islands (e.g., Petal Paradise, Desert Island, Volcano Island) and grants access to advanced features such as flower farming, oil drills, gem mines, and lava pools.
- **Progression Path:** Each Prestige tier expands gameplay depth, requiring higher Bumpkin levels and more resources to progress.
- **Strategic Choice:** Players must decide when to Prestige, balancing the loss of current progress against the benefits of unlocking new content.

**Irreversible:** Once Prestige is activated, all prior progress is reset and cannot be recovered.

### What are good expansion strategies in Sunflower Land?
Expanding land in Sunflower Land requires careful planning of resources, time, and Bumpkin progression. Since each stage consumes coins and materials while taking real-time to unlock, players benefit from managing farming cycles and resource gathering efficiently.

  - Mine stone, iron, and gold as soon as nodes are available.  
  - Balance crop planting with resource gathering to avoid shortages.

  - Start longer expansions (8–24 hours) before logging off so they complete while inactive.

  - Cook meals and feed your Bumpkin to gain XP faster.

- **Balance economy:**  
  - Sell surplus crops for coins to fund expansions.  
  - Avoid spending all resources on crafting; reserve enough for upcoming unlocks.

## Resources

### How do I gather resources in Sunflower Land?
Resources are the foundation of expansions and crafting in Sunflower Land. Each type of resource comes from specific nodes on your land and requires the right tools to collect.

**Wood**
- Gathered by chopping trees on your land.
- Trees regenerate after being chopped, allowing continuous collection.
- Used for crafting tools and early expansions.

**Stone**
- Mined from Stone Rocks using a **Wood Pickaxe**.
- Unlocks once you craft your first pickaxe from wood.
- Required for expansions, crafting stone tools, and building upgrades.

**Iron**
- Mined from Iron Rocks using a **Stone Pickaxe**.
- Appears in later expansions (Petal Paradise and Desert Island).
- Needed for advanced expansions, crafting iron tools, and oil drills.

**Gold**
- Mined from Gold Rocks using an **Iron Pickaxe**.
- Found in Desert Island and Volcano Island expansions.
- Used for high-level expansions, crafting gold tools, and rare upgrades.

**Obsidian**
- Mined from **lava pits** on Volcano Island using a **Gold Pickaxe**.
- Used for advanced crafting and exchanging into Sunstones.
- Becomes available only in late-game expansions.

**Sunstone**
- Mined from **Sunstone nodes** using a **Gold Pickaxe**.  
- Each node can be mined 10 times before disappearing permanently.  
- Additional Sunstones can be obtained by exchanging **3 Obsidian → 1 Sunstone** with Gorga in the Infernos (requires Bumpkin Level 30).  
- Used for late-game mechanics such as node forging.

**Resource progression path**
1. Chop trees → collect wood.  
2. Craft **Wood Pickaxe** → mine stone.  
3. Craft **Stone Pickaxe** → mine iron.  
4. Craft **Iron Pickaxe** → mine gold.  
5. Mine **Obsidian** from lava pits (Volcano Island).  
6. Mine **Sunstones** from nodes or exchange Obsidian in Infernos.  

**Irreversible:** Once resources are spent on expansions or crafting, they cannot be refunded. Sunstone nodes are finite and disappear permanently once fully mined.

### What is the Infernos in Sunflower Land?
The Infernos is a late-game map area in Sunflower Land, unlocked once your Bumpkin reaches Level 30. It introduces volcanic environments, rare resources, and goblin exchanges that are essential for advanced progression.

**Access**

**Gameplay impact**
- Obsidian and Sunstone are required for late-game crafting and node forging.  
- Sunstone nodes are finite, making resource management critical.  
- Infernos expands the resource economy beyond coins, wood, stone, iron, and gold.

**Irreversible:** Sunstone nodes disappear permanently once fully mined. Obsidian and Sunstone spent on exchanges or crafting cannot be refunded.

## Cooking FAQ

### What is Cooking in Sunflower Land?
Cooking is the primary way to level up your Bumpkin. Unlike other farming games, experience is not earned from harvesting or deliveries — only from eating food. Cooking also supports deliveries, quests, and advanced progression.

**Cooking Buildings**
- **Firepit** – First cooking building (free). Simple recipes with short cooking times. Skills: *Fast Feasts* (–10% time), *Fiery Jackpot* (20% chance to double meals).  
- **Kitchen** – Most versatile building with the largest recipe set, including fish and greenhouse produce. Some recipes require other cooked foods. Skills: *Fast Feasts* (–10% time).  
- **Smoothie Shack** – Produces juices and blended drinks from fruits/vegetables (sometimes Eggs). Skills: *Juicy Boost* (+10% XP from drinks).  
- **Deli** – Advanced building for fermented products and long-cook foods. Produces Cheese for high-level recipes. Skills: *Drive-Through Deli* (+10% XP).  
- **Bakery** – Bakes cakes and pastries using Eggs, wheat, and vegetables. High XP but costly ingredients. Skills: *Frosted Cakes* (–10% time).  

**Cooking Mechanics**
- Only one recipe can cook per building at a time.  
- VIP players can queue up to 3 extra recipes per building.  
- Recipes consume ingredients immediately when queued, but canceling before cooking returns them.  
- Gems can speed up cooking; cost decreases as recipes near completion.  

**Cooking with Oil**
- Unlockable after Desert Island and Oil Wells.  
- Oil reduces cooking times across buildings.  
- Runtime added per unit of oil varies:  
  - Firepit: 24 hours  
  - Kitchen: 4h 48m  
  - Smoothie Shack: 3h  
  - Deli: 2h  
  - Bakery: 2h 24m  
- Oil is expensive, so best used during competitions or for high-demand recipes.  

**Gameplay impact**
- Eating cooked food is the only way to gain XP and level up.  
- Higher-level recipes are required for advanced deliveries and quests.  
- Cooking skills and oil boosts make progression faster and more efficient.
