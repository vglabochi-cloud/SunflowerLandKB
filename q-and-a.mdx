### How can I withdraw $FLOWER?
You can withdraw $FLOWER by going to the Retreat (click the Map icon) and walking up to the goblin with a wallet icon. Click the $FLOWER icon and choose the network you‚Äôd like to receive your tokens on ‚Äî either **Ronin** or **Base Network**.

### Do I need ETH to withdraw?
Yes, you need ETH to cover the gas fees when withdrawing $FLOWER. You can purchase ETH through exchanges like Binance or Coinbase, or directly from the wallet provider you‚Äôre using.  
Make sure to buy and transfer ETH on the **Base Network**, since only ETH on Base is accepted for withdrawals.

### How to fix "shared network error" on withdrawal?
If you see a shared network error, you need to wait **14 days** from when the error first appeared.  
This waiting time is required by the system‚Äôs security process, so retrying earlier will not work.

---

### When was the "shared network error" implemented?
The shared network error was implemented on **January 12, 2026**.  
This security measure was added to prevent abuse of withdrawals and ensure fair play. If you encounter this error, you‚Äôll need to wait the required **14‚Äëday period** before trying again.

---

### Why do I get a "shared network error" if my network isn‚Äôt shared?
Even if your network isn‚Äôt shared, most Internet service providers use **dynamic IP addresses**. This means your IP address can change over time and may be temporarily assigned to another user.  
If another player using the same IP has already made a withdrawal, the system will detect it as a shared network and apply the **14‚Äëday waiting period**.  
This rule exists to prevent multiple withdrawals from the same IP in a short time, ensuring fairness and security.

---

### Do I need to change my network/IP before waiting two weeks for a withdrawal?
No, you don‚Äôt need to change your IP address or network settings. The important step is to wait the full **two‚Äëweek period** before trying another withdrawal.  
This waiting time is part of the system‚Äôs security checks, so changing your network won‚Äôt speed it up.

**What to do:**  
- Try increasing the gas limit and/or gas price.  
- Check if you have pending transactions and clear them.  
- Ensure you‚Äôre connected to a reliable RPC endpoint.  
- If the issue persists, wait until network congestion eases and retry.

## üåê Network Transactions FAQ

### I have enough gas fee but I cannot proceed with the transaction. Why is that?
Even if you have enough ETH to cover gas fees, transactions can still fail or get stuck for several reasons:

- **Network congestion**: When many people are using base, transactions may remain pending or fail if the gas price is too low compared to current demand.  
- **Gas limit too low**: If the gas limit is set below what the transaction requires, it can run out of gas before completion and fail.  
- **Nonce conflicts**: Ethereum uses a ‚Äúnonce‚Äù (sequence number) for each transaction. If you have pending or stuck transactions, new ones may not process until the earlier ones are cleared.  
- **Smart contract errors**: If you‚Äôre interacting with a contract (e.g., marketplace, farming logic), the contract itself may reject the transaction even if gas is sufficient.  
- **Wallet/network issues**: Wallets (like MetaMask) or RPC endpoints sometimes experience delays or errors, causing transactions to fail despite sufficient gas.   

---

### Why does my transaction stay pending even though I have enough gas?
Pending transactions often happen due to **network congestion**. If many users are submitting transactions at the same time, your transaction may wait in the mempool until miners/validators pick it up.  
- If your gas price is lower than the current market rate, it may remain pending for a long time.  
- You can speed it up by increasing the gas price or using the ‚Äúspeed up‚Äù option in your wallet.

---

### What should I do if my transaction is stuck?
1. Check if you have **pending transactions** in your wallet.  
2. Use the **‚Äúcancel‚Äù or ‚Äúspeed up‚Äù** option in your wallet to clear or replace the stuck transaction.  
3. Increase the **gas price** to match current network demand.  
4. Switch to a more reliable **RPC endpoint** if your current one is slow or overloaded.  
5. Wait until **network congestion eases** and retry.
